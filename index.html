This week's spotlight is Aerial Photography.

Tell us about yourself. What’s your background?

Professionally, I’m a Web Developer and I used to run my own photography business called Lance Anderson Photography. I primarily did weddings and family stuff, as well as corporate events. I still enjoy photography as a hobby.

What do you look for when scoping out subjects for your photos?

I’m really into architecture. When I travel I look for symmetric, geometrical patterns and minimalism. That’s naturally what my eye seeks out when I’m shooting.

Do you have a favorite destination you’ve traveled to?

I would have to say that Barcelona is the most beautiful city I’ve ever been to. I traveled there before I was a professional photographer, so I had a lot to learn on the photography front. If I went back now I would probably take much better photos.

Barcelona was really incredible though. They’re well known for their beautiful architecture. There are really unique buildings there. Park Guell is known for their statues and there’s a lot of intricate tile work around the city.

Tell us more about the photo you captured of Chicago’s Aqua Building.

I definitely found it while exploring. I’m pretty familiar with the area around Millennium Park in downtown Chicago. One of my favorite spots is to shoot by the Aquarium where you can see the downtown skyline and Navy Pier on your left.

I took this photo while I was in town visiting the city and I was just wandering. I didn’t even know what the building was called at the time. It just had a really interesting exterior.

What type of camera do you shoot on?

A couple years ago I switched to the Fuji X-T2 Mirrorless system. Previously I had been shooting Nikon DSLR. I didn’t like the size and bulk of DSLR anymore since I was shooting as a hobby. Since I travel, switching to mirrorless allows me to bring lenses, cards, and the camera body in one bag. The technology has come a long way too.

Do you use any software to edit your photos?

I upload my images into Adobe Lightroom and do some basic editing there to adjust contrast, brightness, curves. If I want to do more advanced stuff I upload them into Photoshop. I’m sure it’s pretty obvious, but the sky in my photo is pretty heavily edited. I masked the building and sky and added a gradient and curve adjustment to get the building to separate from the background.

If you could pick one destination where you’d like to shoot, where would you travel to?

I’d definitely like to go back to Barcelona, but there’s one area I’d like to go to where I haven’t been to yet and that’s the Seattle area. I’d like to go to the Pacific Northwest to get some photos of the coastline and the trees. There are lots of beautiful hiking areas in the mountains out there.
